About Me

Building Systems That Help People —
and Getting the Balance Right

I’m a student at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Science with a minor in Data Science. My interests center on the intersection of data analytics, information systems, and the ethical, responsible advancement of technology.

I’m motivated by the challenge of keeping innovation and responsibility in balance — minimizing harm while maximizing the positive impact technology can have on society. My goal is to build the technical fluency and analytical mindset needed to contribute to smarter, fairer systems that serve people effectively. Whether through data analysis, system design, or information architecture, I aim for my work to create real, measurable value.

Before transferring to UNC, I completed coursework in Finance and Management Information Systems at the Rochester Institute of Technology, where I built a foundation in both business strategy and technical problem-solving.

Ultimately, my goal is to build and contribute to systems that meaningfully impact society, using data and AI not just to optimize performance, but to drive innovation with integrity and purpose.
