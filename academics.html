Academics

Highlighted coursework from UNC Chapel Hill and Rochester Institute of Technology.

INLS 201 UNC Chapel Hill

Foundations of Information Science

Surveyed the evolution and scope of information science, covering how information is represented, organized, retrieved, and governed. Examined human information-seeking behavior, digital information policy, and scholarly communication — foundational context for understanding both the design of data systems and the governance structures that shape how technology affects people.

Information Policy Data Governance Ethics
PHIL 143 UNC Chapel Hill

AI and the Future of Humanity

Analyzed the philosophical and societal implications of artificial intelligence, virtual reality, and biological augmentation — including questions about AI risk and benefit, algorithmic bias, moral constraints on AI systems, and the relationship between human and machine decision-making. Strengthened my ability to anticipate the downstream effects of the systems I help build.

AI Ethics Algorithmic Bias Responsible AI Philosophy of Technology
INLS 523 UNC Chapel Hill

Introduction to Database Concepts & Applications

Designed and implemented relational database systems using SQL, with emphasis on semantic modeling, normalization, indexing, and query construction. Database architecture is the infrastructure layer underlying analytics pipelines, machine learning systems, and data products — understanding it deeply is essential to building technology that actually works at scale and can be trusted.

SQL Relational Modeling Data Systems
COMP 110 UNC Chapel Hill

Introduction to Programming

Built foundational Python programming skills with a focus on problem decomposition, algorithms for data analysis, abstraction design, and computing ethics. Emphasized applying computational thinking to real-world problems — directly supporting data science workflows and building an understanding of how the design choices embedded in code carry real consequences for real people.

Python Algorithms Computing Ethics
MATH 115 UNC Chapel Hill

Reasoning with Data: Navigating a Quantitative World

Applied mathematical and statistical methods to authentic societal problems — including voting systems, public health and risk, personal finance, and human behavior. Sharpened the ability to interpret quantitative evidence critically and communicate data-driven insights clearly, skills that are essential when building systems meant to inform consequential decisions.

Statistical Reasoning Quantitative Analysis Societal Applications
POLI 281 UNC Chapel Hill

Data in Politics I: An Introduction

Examined the transformative effect of the Information Revolution on policy, journalism, law, and public decision-making. Developed hands-on skills in handling and analyzing data to address important social problems. This course deepened my understanding of how data shapes power — and how responsible, accurate analysis is critical to systems that are meant to serve the public interest.

Data Analysis Public Policy Civic Applications
STAT 146 Rochester Institute of Technology

Statistics II

Developed advanced inferential statistics skills including multiple regression, analysis of variance (ANOVA), hypothesis testing, and predictive modeling using Minitab. These methods form the quantitative backbone of data science — enabling evidence-based conclusions from complex datasets and the ability to evaluate whether a model's outputs should actually be trusted.

Regression Analysis ANOVA Predictive Modeling Minitab
MGIS 130 Rochester Institute of Technology

Information Systems & Technology

Explored enterprise information systems, IT governance frameworks, and the strategic integration of technology with business operations. Understanding how organizations manage information infrastructure — including security, compliance, and system design — builds the operational context needed to create technology that functions well within real-world institutional constraints.

IT Strategy Systems Analysis Compliance
MGMT 340 Rochester Institute of Technology

Business Ethics and Corporate Social Responsibility

Examined ethical frameworks applied to business at both macro and micro levels — covering stakeholder theory, corporate governance, product safety, employee rights, environmental responsibility, and ethical leadership. Directly relevant to the challenge of building technology responsibly: the gap between innovation and accountability is exactly the kind of problem this course trained me to identify, reason through, and address.

Business Ethics Stakeholder Theory Corporate Governance Responsible Innovation
