SmartSip Incorporated — The Smart Hydration Bottle

CEO & Co-Founder · Team: Kiera Moran · Evan Ren · Sai Bhuyan

As part of RIT Saunders' signature Business 2 (MGMT 102) Freshman Experience — a course where first-year teams develop a full venture from ideation through commercialization and pitch to a panel of alumni judges and industry entrepreneurs — I served as CEO of SmartSip Incorporated, a four-person startup team that designed and pitched a smart hydration bottle targeting collegiate athletes and active adults aged 20–35.

The SmartSip bottle integrates a real-time hydration sensor, Bluetooth sync with Apple Watch and Fitbit, a gamified mobile app with hydration streaks and social challenges, and a Smart LED reminder ring — all in a double-wall insulated stainless steel form factor. The team developed a complete business plan covering market sizing (TAM: $26.4B), competitor benchmarking against HidrateSpark, LARQ, and others, a B2B and D2C go-to-market strategy targeting college athletics programs, and a financial model projecting $531,993 in gross profit from 6,650 units at a $79.99 price point with ~40% margins.
